This is a list of offenders executed in the United States in 2017 . As of October 7, 2017, nineteen criminal offenders have been executed in the U.S. in 2017. In addition, eleven further executions are scheduled throughout the year. [1]

Double execution in Arkansas [ edit ]

See also: Capital punishment in Arkansas and List of individuals executed in Arkansas

On April 24, 2017, Arkansas carried out back-to-back executions. Convicted rapist and murderer Jack Jones, age 52, was pronounced dead at 7:20 pm Monday. Approximately three hours later, convicted rapist and murderer Marcel Williams, age 46, was pronounced dead at 10:33 pm. Jones was sentenced to death for the 1995 rape and murder of Mary Phillips (August 18, 1959 - June 6, 1995) and the near-fatal assault of her then-11-year-old daughter, Lacy Phillips (born c. May 1984), during a botched robbery in Bald Knob, Arkansas. Williams was sent to death row for the 1994 rape and murder of 22-year-old Stacy Errickson (December 15, 1971 - c. November 20, 1994), whom he kidnapped from a gas station in central Arkansas. [26]

The last double execution in Arkansas was on September 8, 1999. [27] By conducting the double execution in 2017, Arkansas became the first U.S. state to put more than one inmate to death on the same day in 17 years. The last state to do so was Texas, which executed two murderers in August 2000 . Oklahoma planned a double execution in 2014 but scrapped plans for the second one after the first (the execution of Clayton Lockett ) went awry. [28]

Arkansas executed four men in an eight-day period in 1960. The only quicker pace included quadruple executions in 1926 and 1930. [28]
