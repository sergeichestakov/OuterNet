The Merișor derailment was a railway accident that took place on 8 April 2017 at Merișor ( ro ) , Bănița , Romania on the Tîrgu Jiu to Simeria line, killing both crew members on board. [1] [2]

Accident [ edit ]

The map of the accident location

When the accident occurred the train was en route from Slatina to Curtici , carrying waste iron. Whilst en-route from Petroşani to Subcetate , near the village of Pui, the train driver and helper noticed that the train was speeding uncontrollably and whilst trying to slow down, the train drivers also noticed the brake systems failed. The runway train derailed on a bridge in a curve after overspeeding, killing both train drivers and destroying most of the consist in the process. The damage was severe. The locomotive was severely damaged whilst most of the freight cars got destroyed, falling into a river ten meters below. The railway line also suffered damage too, with most of the trackside objects and OHLE being destroyed in the process.

Most passenger trains (Semi Fast and Fast trains) were redirected on the Filiaşi - Orşova - Timisoara - Arad line (Mainline 900) whilst slower stopping services going to Simeria will stop at the nearest rail station, then transported by coaches on the disrupted section, and put back on the train at the nearest station on the other side of the accident.

The Romanian Railways announced that they will bring 4 heavy cranes to clean up the aftermath of the accident.

Investigation [ edit ]

Based on the first investigation, it appears that the train had a problem in the braking system. [3]
