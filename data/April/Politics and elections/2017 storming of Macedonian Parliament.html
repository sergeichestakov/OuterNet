Storming of the Macedonian Parliament (also known as Bloody Thursday ) happened on 27 April 2017, when about 200 Macedonian nationalists stormed the Macedonian Parliament in reaction to the election of Talat Xhaferi , an ethnic Albanian , as speaker of the Assembly of the Republic of Macedonia. [5] The violence was condemned by the European Union and NATO , who also greeted the election of Xhaferi as new parliament speaker. [6]

Background [ edit ]

Macedonia has been involved in political turmoil in recent years, cumulating in mass anti government protests in 2015 and 2016 . These protests are the result of corruption allegations, which are the main cause of Macedonia's political crisis, against VMRO leader Nikola Gruevski and other VMRO officials. [7] They are further accused of preventing a new government forming, following the parliamentary election in 2016 , in order to prevent losing power and facing prosecution for corruption. [7] There have been daily protests across the nation by supporters of the VMRO-DPMNE party. [1]

The conflict also has ethnic undertones, as the reason no coalition government has been formed was due to the demand for the Albanian language to be the second official language in Government of Macedonia, and due to attempts by opposition leader Zoran Zaev to form a coalition government with Albanian ethnic parties. [5] Macedonia has seen mass ethnic violence recently, particularly in 2001 and 2012 .

Incident [ edit ]

Around 200 protestors stormed the parliament building after Xhaferi was elected speaker. Many were masked and they threw chairs and punches at journalists and MPs, injuring SDSM leader Zoran Zaev . [5] Radmila Sekerinska , deputy head of the SDSM, required stitches after being dragged by her hair. [7] Police had to deploy stun grenades to break up the mob. [1]
