On Saturday, February 18, 2017, 20,000 Catholic people gathered in Manila , Philippines , in what was dubbed by the Catholic Bishops' Conference of the Philippines (CBCP) as a " Walk for Life " to oppose the reintroduction of the death penalty and call for an end to killings amid the country's ongoing "war on drugs" initiated by President Rodrigo Duterte . The church had led the revolutions that successfully toppled former Presidents Ferdinand Marcos in 1986 and Joseph Estrada in 2001 . [2]

Background [ edit ]

On January 30, 2017, the Catholic Bishops' Conference of the Philippines (CBCP) issued a statement opposing the revival of the death penalty. [3] CBCP President Socrates Villegas called on the Filipino people to join a rally, which the CBCP had called a "Walk for Life", opposing the killings amid the country's war on drugs. The rally was set for February 18, 2017. [3]

Event [ edit ]

On February 18, 2017, about 20,000 people (or 10,000: by police estimate) joined the Saturday event. [4] The "Walk for Life" was organized after the Catholic Church expressed alarm over the killings of drug users in the country's war on drugs since Rodrigo Duterte took office as President on June 30, 2016. [4] Among those who joined the rally was Senator Leila de Lima , a critic of Duterte who opposes the war on drugs [5] and who was later charged with participation in the drug trade and imprisoned at Camp Crame .

On July 2, 2017, after the subsequent killings of drug suspect in Caloocan , 500 Catholics joined the march, calling to stop the killings for those person who included in the drugs watchlist. The Diocese of Caloocan said that the killings are not the solution of wiping the drugs in the country. [6]
