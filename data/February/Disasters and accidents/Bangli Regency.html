Bangli

Bangli is a regency ( kabupaten ) of Bali , Indonesia . It has an area of 520.81 km 2 and population of 197,210 (2004). Its regency seat is Bangli .

Up till 1907, Bangli was one of the nine kingdoms [ citation needed ] of Bali. The capital has a famous Hindu temple, the Pura Kehen , which dates from the 11th century. Bangli also has one village, lies surround a hill, Demulih.

The northern part of the district included the main road to the north coast passing through Kintamani and around the crater in which Gunung Batur sits. [1]

From the Demulih hill, Bali Island, particularly its southern part can be seen – Kuta , Nusa Dua , Gianyar and a part of Klungkung .

Bangli is the only regency in Bali which landlocked.

Administration [ edit ]

The regency is divided into four districts ( kecamatan ):

  • Susut
  • Bangli
  • Tembuku
  • Kintamani
