On 23 August 2017, the Kaifiyat Express train derailed between Pata and Achalda railway stations in Auraiya district of Uttar Pradesh , India causing multiple injuries. [1] This is second train derailment within a week in Uttar Pradesh after Kalinga Utkal Express derailed near Khatauli on 19 August 2017 in Muzaffarpur district . [2]

Accident [ edit ]

Train collided with a dump truck on the line and 10 coaches of Kaifiyat Express derailed. At least 74 persons injured, four in critical condition. Critical are shifted to Etawah district hospital.

It is said that that dumper belongs to the railway department and carried sand for the railway work of dedicated freight corridor running parallel to the track, however, railway officials in Delhi said the dumper did not belong to the corridor project.

Aftermath [ edit ]

National Disaster Response Force personnel were sent to join the rescue operation at the scene.

All trains to this route are cancelled until the rescue operations are finished and track is restored.

Response [ edit ]

Railway minister Suresh Prabhu said the injured have been shifted to nearby hospitals.

After this accident India's Railways minister Suresh Prabhu offered to quit from his post; however, Prime Minister Narendra Modi asked him to wait further. [3]
