The "GTA Gang" ( Russian : банда ГТА ; Banda GTA ) was the nickname given to a violent gang of murderers and terrorists near Moscow , Russia . The gang would use homemade caltrops to stop vehicles on the Federal Automobile Road M-4 and murder the occupants. Russian media dubbed them the "GTA gang" after the video game Grand Theft Auto , in reference to the violent behavior exhibited by the gang members. In August 2017, four members fled internationally and have yet to be located. [1] In the same month, three members also died in an attempt to flee court custody. [2]

According to investigators, Ibaydullo Subkhanov formed the group in March 2012 to train soldiers for the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL). Over the course of two and a half years, the gang has been responsible for seventeen murders and two serious injuries in the Moscow and Kaluga Oblasts . Russian media began warning travelers about the gang in the summer of 2014, [3] comparing their violent robberies with the actions of characters in the Grand Theft Auto video game series. Police agencies issued warnings to travelers, advising them not to stop until they reached a police road post or petrol station.

Timeline of events [ edit ]

Early activity [ edit ]

The gang was founded in March 2012 by Kyrgyz national Ibaydullo Subkhanov. During that time, he was living in the village of Udelnaya as a self-proclaimed " jamaat ", with the purpose of training soldiers for Isil. Subkhanov, operating under the alias Rustam Usmanov, believed a revolution would soon occur in Uzbekistan and Islamic soldiers would be needed in the fight. He would bring new members along with him on "missions" as a form of training, and forced new members to kill victims of their crimes as a way of proving their dedication to the cause.

In September 2013, a motorist was killed along the M9 highway . The following month, another man was killed near the town of Istra and in December, the bodies of four men were discovered in a cabin north of the capital, all suffering from gunshot wounds.

Late into the night of May 2, 2014, an elderly couple, Anatoly and Tatyana Lebedev, were ambushed and killed along the road. Though an iPad was reported stolen, no other valuables were taken despite a wallet being clearly visible on the dashboard.

On 30 June 2014, Alexei Tsyganov, a 53-year-old man from Tula Oblast , was found shot dead 25 meters from his car 45 km from Moscow.

On 18 August 2014, a fourth victim of the gang's activities, 31 year old Albert Yusupov, was found dead along the a-108 motorway. He had called a friend about his burst tyre hours before his body was found. The story attracted widespread attention in the media, as all victims were killed with 9mm bullets and their vehicles had been damaged by homemade caltrops . [3] [4] [5]

On November 6th, 2014, police raided an estate on Gor'kov Street in Udelnaya, resulting in the death of Subkhanov and the arrest of nine other gang members. The accused began their trials in 2016.

Kholik Subkhanov, brother of Ibaydullo, was killed alongside Abdumukin Mamadchinov and Mirzomavlon Mirzosharipov during a failed attempt to escape from a Moscow courthouse on August 1st, 2017. Gang members Umar Khasanov and Zafarjon Gulyamov were also injured.

Vigilante response [ edit ]

Vigilante citizens, outraged by a lack of action from police and other authorities, soon organized armed patrols and started night raids in the areas south of Moscow, checking suspicious cars in an effort to stop the gang. This kind of reckless and unlawful activity made authorities nervous and the police pleaded for people not to interfere with law enforcement operations. [6] [7] [8]

Police eventually issued the generated picture [9] of a suspect by the middle of September 2014: [10] [11] [12] Different methods were used by police and other authorities to find the gang, including the stolen iPad signal tracking: [13] and switching off traffic lights . [14] [15]

Membership [ edit ]

Leader [ edit ]

Ibaydullo Subkhanov (sometimes known as Rustam Usmanov, an alias), was a citizen of Osh, Kyrgyzstan , but is Uzbek by ethnicity. [8] He came to Russia sometime in 2004 and found a place to live in a servant house by the villa in the village of Udelnaya near Moscow . An older lady named Barakat Khasanova already lived in the house by that time. Later, a younger woman named Ranohon Khasanova moved in. She is supposed to be his unofficial wife, and was accompanied by two daughters from previous relationships. [16] Ranohon Khasanova and her children disappeared before or after the firefight on the night of November 6th and are still listed internationally. Subkhanov became a radical Islamist in his home country in 2011 and later fought in the Syrian Civil War on the ISIL side. Subkhanov came back to Russia and met some Tajiks , members of extremist pan-Islamic organization “ Hizb ut-Tahrir ” which is prohibited in Russia. Later Subkhanov had been working as a house/villa servant for a top official in the federal office of the Prosecutor General of Russia , Alexei Staroverov, who has owned the villa since 2010. Subkhanov's mother also reportedly worked in the official's home. He was killed by a police sniper during a gunfight on 6 November 2014 at 32 years old.

Media speculation [ edit ]

Several theories on the gang's origins circulated in Russian media. Some expressed a belief that the killers were Satanists on account of the black, crucifix-shaped caltrops used in the attacks. [17] [18]

Vladimir Zhirinovsky , leader of the Liberal Democratic Party , theorised the gangsters were Ukranian nationalists attempting subsversive tactics against the Russian state. [19]

Well known psychologist Mikhail Vinogradov (who worked in the police force during the Soviet era ) said that the investigation team got some photos and videos from surveillance cameras . The general impression of the gang leader and two helpers gave a clue that they are moving too swiftly, as they are experienced professionals from special forces or former members of some authority. [20]

Fight and capture [ edit ]

On 6 November 2014, the news came that gang had been captured after a fierce gunfight during the night of 5/6 November 2014. [21] [22] This took place around 3:00-3:20 in a private villa ( 55°37′30″N 38°01′33″E ﻿ / ﻿ 55.6250354°N 38.0257228°E ﻿ / 55.6250354; 38.0257228 ) situated at Gor'kov street, 39, in the village of Udelnaya, Ramensky District , 10–15 km south-east of Moscow. The gang leader was killed during the fight. He had initiated the shooting by throwing a hand grenade towards the police and FSB special team crying “ Allahu Abkar ” and was immediately shot by a sniper.

Aftermath [ edit ]

Around 60 units of weapons were found after the gunfire was stopped and a fire was controlled by firefighters. Most of the firearms were gas pistols (a non-lethal weapon for self-defense) remade to fire live bullets from standard 9mm ammunition rounds. All of these pistols had stickers with numbers. Experts found that one of these pistols was used in 6 murders. [8]

Another 10 people were captured in other places in the Moscow region . On November 6th, the main group of the GTA gang was captured at Dmitrovskoe highway, north of Moscow. Two Kalashnikov submachine guns (AKS type) with 100 rounds were confiscated. [23] Some gang members were on the run until the 16th of November. [24] [25]

List of suspects (Surname, First Name) - 7-8 males, 2-3 females (according to different sources):

Males: Absmatov Rashid, Okhunov Nematjohn, Rakhimov Abnunakhob, Ibragimov Dierbek, Akhmadjonov Alerbek, Sultanov Tolibjohn, Mamadchonov Abdumukim, Fazalitdin Khasanov. Khazratkhion Dodokhoev and Zafarjohn Guljamov were arrested later

Females: Sultanova Khosiyat, Sultanova Markhabo, Chekushina Maria (Russian citizen, later released as the wife of another labor migrant, not connected with the gang) [8] [21] [26]

Later, two other people were arrested in Tajikistan by local police: the main ideologist of the gang, Bair Gulomov, who is the uncle of fellow gang member Zafarjohn Guljamov, was arrested around November 2015, and IS recruiter, 38 year old Farrukh Azamjohn (information came 28 June 2016). As of June 2016, the Russian authorities were still in consultation with authorities in Tajikistan about getting both suspects under Russian justice. [27] [28] [29] [30] [31]

President's congratulations [ edit ]

Russian President Vladimir Putin himself congratulated the minister of internal affairs Vladimir Kolokol'cev with a successful investigation against the gang. Putin mentioned that the gang's action can be counted as a terrorist crime. He also applauded the cooperation between the police and Federal Security Service (FSB) during the investigation and said "Hope you will get it (investigation) to the end." [32] [33] [34]

ISIL connected terrorist group [ edit ]

The information about the GTA gang and their connection with ISIL later leaked into the mass-media in Russia, in Islam news, [35] and abroad: [36] The gang leader Subkhanov was probably previously personally connected with ISIL, and kept contact with ISIL after his participation in the Syrian war. He got help from the main ideologist of a gang, Bair Gulomov (Tajik), [37] who found and encouraged newcomers. Meanwhile, war-experienced Ibaydullo Subkhanov trained and ruled over all other GTA gang members, planned and executed most assaults with at least one newcomer as an "entrance exam" to the gang. [38] [39] [40] [41]

The gang members, mainly Tajiks and Uzbeks by ethnicity, citizens of Tajikistan , Uzbekistan and Kyrgyzstan , and labor migrant mainly occupied as unofficial illegal taxi drivers, called themselves "jaamat" ( Jamia is a group in Arabic ) and were on a mission to hunt down " infidels " on the motor roads as a fast and efficient way of training future Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant fighters. [42] [43] [44]

A few kilos of RDX (hexogen) explosives were found in the GTA gang hiding places which indicated that the “jaamat” was ready to commit a terror act in the future. [45] [46]

Motive [ edit ]

On November 12th, the head representative of the Investigative Committee of Russia (IC or SK in Russian) Vladimir Markin suddenly said: “ Version that the gang members played like the heroes of the computer play GTA has no grounds. Our investigation team found out that the aims of the murders did by the gang members were just lucrative, they rob the money of victims ”. He did not specify when and how much money they took. These discrepancies in the gang's motives were widely discussed in Russia and abroad [47] [48] , but not in official mainstream media in Russia, except in an article in which the connection between the labor migrant hate (caused by their low and unstable salary, slave-like working conditions and unprotected semi-official half-illegal life in Russia) towards the Russian citizens and their eager willingness to serve ISIL was discussed. [49]

Top functionary from Prosecutor Office involved [ edit ]

Alexei Staroverov, head of the Prosecutor General of Russia Office administration, has been suspended temporarily while an internal investigation is carried out, his office confirmed on November 12th, 2014. [50] One day later, the head of IC Markin confirmed that Alexey Staroverov was questioned by IC as a witness regarding the GTA gang. The criminal case about illegal possession of weapons and ammunition found in his villa (article 222, Criminal Code of Russia ) was initiated against him on 13 November. [51]

His and his wife's income combined was more than 21.419+26.6=48 million rubles, but the villa (273 sq. meters) itself and the land around it (1110 sq. meters) officially belongs to their underage son, a typical way to hide capital and realty, from the purchase year 2010. [52] [53] Deputy Prosecutor General Viktor Grin had briskly intervened, issuing a personal order to close the criminal case against Alexei Staroverov as a harsh move in a long-running conflict between the Russian Investigative Committee and Prosecutor General's Office. [54] [55] Alexey Staroverov became ill and was hospitalized with cardiology problems around 20 November. He had also applied for resignation. The head of the Cardiology Department where Staroverov spent some time recovering was Dr. Konstantin Lyadov, who was also included in the list of witnesses questioned about the GTA gang by IC. Konstantin Lyadov owns a villa at village Pozdnyakovo, Krasnogorsk district, where another member of the GTA gang, Fazalitdin Khasanov (a brother of the gang leader's wife) made weapons (including remaking gas pistols) and caltrops for his friends. [56] [57] [58] Russian opposition activist Alexei Navalny used the GTA gang connection to Staroverov to blame the corrupted prosecutors with cover-up of ISIL terrorists in his famous anti-corruption video named “ Chaika ” (which means "gull" in Russian) after the acting Prosecutor General of Russia Yury Chaika . [59]

Official report [ edit ]

SK/ IC failed to provide any kind of list of the valuables which are supposed to have been a lucrative aim for the murders. [60] On 21 October 2015, the leader of Russian IC Markin gave an interview to TV channel “Russia 24” in which he stated again that the aims of the gang were purely lucrative: "For a long time could not understand the reasons for a long time there were a lot of rumors about what they were guided. But the motives were commonplace – a seizure of property and money." He did not mention any amount of money or valuable property taken. [61]

He also stated that the 17 victims were killed and that 4 more gang members had disappeared abroad and were still being searched for (internationally listed). [1]

A few previous murders which occurred in 2009 in Moscow (but not on the highway) [62] and in November–December 2013 in the Ryazan region were found to be connected with the GTA gang and are now under investigation. [26]

On 28 June 2016, the criminal case came to the Moscow region's high court. [63]

Trial [ edit ]

On 6 July 2016, the Moscow regional court settled the preliminary hearing date for 13 July, [64] some news leaked into media. [65] [66] [67]

The preliminary hearing started on 13 July in a closed session and the next session date was set for 19 July. All video and photo recording was banned in the first session at the request of the accused. Jury personnel checked the public to prevent mobile camera recording. Three prosecutors acted for the state. Some of the defense lawyers reported that they had no time to read all 170 volumes of the case. Because of this, Judge Natalia Valikova verified the personality of accused and decided to move all other actions to the next session on 1 August 2016. [68] [69]

All 9 charged offenders listened to the accusation read by the state prosecuting attorney during the session of 1 August 2016. The formal accusation took more than 2 hours to read out. The gang members, their cars, and their home addresses were named, the weaponry list took about 10 minutes to read (two Kalashnikov different caliber submachine guns were mentioned, one TT pistol and numerous self-made pistols were named, but no information on hand grenades was given). [70] The 15 crime episodes were mentioned, some of which were previously unknown to the media. The first two victims, killed in 2012, were revealed to also be guest-workers from abroad working as illegal taxi drivers, personally known to the perpetrators, who were killed by the gang just because of the cars. A homicide crime episode without any robbery or other criminal income was described, in which Vladimir Kirillyuk, the head of the supervisory council from "Flora-Moskva" private bank, was killed bicycling on a side-road one night in 2014. The investigators suspected that he was killed because he had witnessed something dangerous for the gang members, although what he had seen was not mentioned. All charged offenders partially admit their guilt, but decided to give their statements later during each criminal episode hearing. [71]

The next hearing was settled on 8 August, then 19th and 24th. A long break then followed, caused by a defendant lawyer's vacations. [72] [73]

Several hearings went on throughout October. In the hearing on October 31st, Anvar Ulugmuradov gave his statements about one homicide and one homicide attempt, involving victims who were completely unknown to the gang, and no robbery attempt or lucrative motives. The crime was committed on the night of 11–12 June 2013 in the car parked in a forest/park near Mendeleevo settlement, Moscow region. Ulugmuradov gave a confession during the investigation that the leader of the gang forced him to kill absolutely unknown persons for training purposes. The leader said that the revolution would soon start in Uzbekistan and that gang members, as future militants, should prove their commitment and dedication to the gang and to the underground by killing somebody. [74] But Ulugmuradov denied his own confessions about purposes on the court session (just during the current hearing). [75] He confirmed that with a yet unknown gang member "Kirgiz" they found a car in which laughing and music could be heard. The man and woman in the car were intended be killed inside the car by point blank shooting through windows (using a pistol with a self-made suppressor in a form of plastic bottle), but 38-years-old Yulia Baranova managed to survive after two wounds (breast and shoulder) rendered her unconscious. She called her friends after she regained consciousness in the morning and they called the police. She again gave her statements on the hearings, stating that nothing was stolen, and the purposes of the crime were non-lucrative. [76]

Escape attempt after the trial session [ edit ]

Five GTA gang members tried to escape from custody in the elevator of the Moscow regional court on 1 August 2017, around 13:50 Moscow time, just after the usual trial session (which ended around 13:00). [77] Nine prisoners from the gang were divided into two groups after the trial session. The first group of 5 men, accompanied by 2 guards, entered the elevator and moved down from the 4th floor where the session was held. [78] [79] [80] [81]

Video news: [82] [83]

The gang members from this group stopped the lift by swinging and jumping in it (or by pressing STOP button), [84] then attacked the two guards, suffocating the female guard from behind and blocking the male guard in the elevator's corner, catching their pistols ( PM pistol with 4 magazines=32 bullets) and keys for the handcuffs. The male guard managed to cry into the radio and warn others in the building. Approximately half an hour later, the technicians in the building [85] managed to move the elevator remotely to the upper floor where the other trial session had just ended.

Against all prisoner's convoy rules, [86] the five dangerous criminals (trained in the Wahhabi terrorist cell for several years) were guarded in the elevator by only two guards, the woman in her 40s and the man 45 years old. [87] Additionally, the prisoners had their hands cuffed in front of them, not behind, as they are supposed to be during the transfer of dangerous criminals. [88] These facts gave grounds for conspiracy theories that the escape attempt was a plot of the authorities, who allegedly decided to kill the criminals by manipulating them into making an escape attempt. [89]

The fugitives held the man as a hostage and beat and tortured the female guard, named Elizaveta Lukjanova, [90] for up to 15 minutes, [91] until she fell unconscious while they waited for the elevator to move again. [92]

The lift then stopped on the 3rd floor, where three or four special police (OMON) officers who happened to be on that floor in the session hall guarding criminals for another trial about organized crime in Miass city [93] were waiting for the fugitives. The firefight was quite long, and according to witnesses, more than 20 gun shots were fired.

A video of the firefight was published on 3 August 2017. Two policemen took positions in the session hall and one lawyer hid beside them with a chair as an improvised weapon. A prisoner from another trial, Viktor Ardabjevskiy, the former mayor of Miass city accused in contract murders, was still inside the bulletproof glass room, and was forced to stay in this locked safe room and watch the firefight through the glass being just three-four meters away. He is considered to be an important eyewitness to the firefight. [94] [95] Three fugitives from the GTA gang were killed and two seriously wounded. [96] [97] A video of their dead bodies lying on the floor was published before the firefight video. [98]

Aftermath [ edit ]

Two police officers who guarded the fugitives in the elevator were rushed to the hospital with serious injuries, [99] but no bullet wounds. [100] One policeman, who encountered the armed fugitives after the elevator's doors were opened, was shot in the shoulder. [78]

The three fugitives killed in the firefight were Abdumukim Mamadchonov, Mirzomavlon Mirzosharipov, and Kholik Subkhanov (the leader's brother). The two wounded are Khasanov and Gulyamov. [101] The fourth fugitive died in the hospital on 2 August 2017. [102] Later the information about the fourth death was not confirmed by the source inside the medical service. [103]
