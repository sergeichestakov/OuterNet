Floods hit southern Quebec on 3 May 2017. After excessive rainfall, 2,426 residences were flooded, 2,720 people were forced from their homes, and 146 municipalities were affected. [1] [2] Montreal and Laval then declared a state of emergency over the flooding. [3]

Transport Quebec closed several roads on 7 May, including the Galipeault Bridge, due to rising water levels. [3]

As of 10 May, the Canadian military had stationed 2,200 troops in Quebec, as well as dozens of military boats, helicopters, armoured vehicles and engineering equipment, and a naval frigate, [4] the HMCS Montreal . [5]
