Killing of Patricia O'Connor

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation , search

Patricia O'Connor was an Irish woman whose dismembered remains were found scattered along a road in the Wicklow Mountains in June 2017. [1]

Events [ edit ]

Saturday 10 June

At about 19:45 IST two walkers on the Military Road near Enniskerry found human remains. [2] [3] [4] The remains found were a torso and Bray Garda station was investigating. [2] Some 30km of roads in Wicklow were closed off during the search. [5]

Monday 12 June

The Garda Water Unit discovered remains near the Glenmacnass Waterfall near to where they had previously found remains. [5] Defence Forces personnel also found remains near Lough Brea Lower. [5]

Tuesday 13 June

A bag containing the head and hands of the deceased were found and limbs were also discovered. [6] Gardaí confirmed the remains are those of an adult woman. [6] A man in his thirties was arrested in Wexford in connection with the remains. [6]

Wednesday 14 June

The deceased was named locally as Patricia O'Connor, a 61 year old resident of Rathfarnham who was reported missing on the second of June. [1] [7]

Gardaí believe that she was killed with a blow from a blunt instrument at the end of May during a row in Rathfarnham. [1] [7] They also believe that her remains were dismembered in a field outside Kilmuckridge and buried there in a shallow grave for two days before being disinterred and scattered along the Military Road in Wicklow. [1] [7]

Thursday 15 June

A 32 year old man from Rathfarnham was charged with Patricia O'Connors' murder at Tallaght District Court. [8] He was arrested that morning at Bray Garda station and when charged claimed "it was self defence". [8] Justice Patricia McNamara said that due to the gravity of the charge there would be no bail. [8] She remanded the accused to appear at Clover Hill Court via videolink the following Thursday. [8]

Thursday 22 June

The man chargepd with the murder appeared in court via videolink. [9] He spoke only to confirm his name and that he could see the court. [10] Detective Garda David Connolly said they needed formal directions from the Director of Public Prosecutions . [9] Judge Victor Blake remanded the accused to appear in court via videolink four weeks later. [9]

Saturday 2 September

A 73 year old man was arrested in County Meath and two women, aged 19 and 38 were arrested in Dublin. [11] [12] They were held in Bray and Wicklow Garda stations. [11] [12] They were released the following day. [13]

Tuesday 26 September

A 40 year old man was arrested in Dublin and was held at Bray Garda station. [14]
