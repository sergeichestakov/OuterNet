Kratovo ( Russian : Кра́тово ) is an urban locality (a suburban (dacha) settlement ) in Ramensky District of Moscow Oblast , Russia , located 40 kilometers (25 mi) southeast of Moscow . Population: 8,277 ( 2010 Census ) ; [1] 6,855 ( 2002 Census ) ; [2] 6,295 ( 1989 Census ) . [3]

On 10 June 2017, a 50-year-old man opened fire on passers-by in the settlement, killing four people. [4]

Notable people [ edit ]

Yuriy Borzakovskiy , who took home the gold in the men's 800-meter run at the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens , was born in Kratovo. Sergei Eisenstein and Sergei Prokofiev had dachas here.
