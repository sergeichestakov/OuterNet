A landslide occurred at about 5:38 am local time on 24 June 2017 in Diexi , Mao County , Sichuan province in south-western China . It destroyed 40 homes in Xinmo village and killed 10 people, with a further 73 people missing, as of 27 June. A second smaller landslide at around 8:15 pm impeded rescue efforts.

Background [ edit ]

Diexi Lake , 1.9 kilometers from the 2017 landslide site, was created by a landslide over the Minjiang River in the 1933 earthquake.

The location of Mao County between the Tibetan Plateau and the Sichuan Basin is characterized by deep mountain canyons with exposed batholith . In 1933, the landslide site became especially unstable after the 7.3-magnitude Diexi earthquake , whose epicenter was only a few kilometers away from Xinmo village. Major geological hazards occur on almost a yearly basis. [1]

Ngawa Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture , also known as Aba, where Mao County is located, was one of the regions worst affected by the 2008 Sichuan earthquake . During the earthquake, 20,258 people in Aba and 3,933 in Mao County were confirmed killed, and 8,183 people in Mao County were injured. [4] A large factor in the fatality rate during both the 1933 and 2008 earthquakes was the landslides and the subsequent formation of landslide dam lakes. [1] [5] During the 1933 earthquake, a catastrophic landslide lake outburst flood occurred on the Minjiang River , which has its headwaters in Aba Prefecture and runs through the region of the landslide. [5]

The majority of Mao County residents, almost 90%, are of the Qiang ethnicity . Large numbers of students had also returned home from university studies. The landslide site is located near the locally known Songpinggou scenic area, where many tourists were staying in guest houses when the landslide struck. [4]

Landslide [ edit ]

Before and after satellite imagery of the affected area

The landslide occurred in the early morning on 24 June 2017 at about 5:38 am local time (21:38 on 23 June UTC ). It was caused by torrential rain. [6] [7] Forty homes were destroyed in Xinmo village (新磨村), where more than 140 people from at least 62 families were initially feared buried. The landslide also blocked a two-kilometre (1.2 mi) stretch of the Songping Stream (松坪沟), [8] [9] as well as a 1,600 m section of road. [7] Approximately 3 million m 3 (110 million cu ft) [10] to 8 million m 3 (280 million cu ft) of rock was displaced in the landslide. [4] The average elevation within a 5-km radius of the landslide was 2967 m, and the landslide lasted an estimated 100 seconds. [1]

A second smaller landslide around 8:15 pm on 24 June affected rescue work. At least 1,959 rescue and medical workers were involved in the recovery effort. [8] Three people from the same family had been pulled alive from the rubble by the evening of 24 June. [10]

By 10 am on 25 June, 15 people had been confirmed killed, with a further 118 listed as missing. [11] By 2 pm on the same day, the number of missing was reduced to 93, after more people were confirmed to be alive or were rescued. [7]

Aftermath and recovery efforts [ edit ]

At least 300 people who survived the landslide were relocated to facilities in Diexi town, including a school and a hotel. The prefecture's civil affairs bureau sent clothing, two electric generators, 20 tents and 400 quilts to the survivors. [7] [12] [13]
