The 2017 Turin stampede occurred on 3 June 2017 when a firecracker caused panic during a screening of the UEFA Champions League Final in Turin , Italy . One woman died [1] [2] and at least 1,526 people were injured. [3]

Stampede [ edit ]

The incident occurred at approximately 10:30 p.m., about 10 minutes before the end of the game, when panic erupted among those at Piazza San Carlo , leading to a stampede. During the panic, a railing of stairs leading to an underground parking garage gave way, causing additional injuries.

The panic was possibly caused by a sudden bang which may have been the result of a firecracker, and someone shouting that a bomb had gone off may have fueled the panic. [4] The bang and the resulting panic caused fears of a bombing or shooting, which was denied by the authorities. [3]

After the stampede and a subsequent evacuation, the square was left strewn with sneakers which were ripped off people's feet as they ran. [4]

Casualties [ edit ]

At least 1,527 people were injured, including 7 who were seriously injured. [5] On 15 June, less than two weeks after the stampede, a 38-year-old woman died in hospital of her sustained injuries. [2]

Reactions [ edit ]

Some older Juventus fans said the stampede had evoked painful memories of the Heysel Stadium disaster in 1985, when 39 fans, mostly Italian, were killed by a collapsing wall in Brussels , Belgium , before the start of a match between Juventus and Liverpool . [4]
