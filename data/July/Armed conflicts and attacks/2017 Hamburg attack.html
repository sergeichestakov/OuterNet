The 2017 Hamburg attack was a mass stabbing that took place on 28 July 2017 in Hamburg , Germany .

Attack [ edit ]

At 3pm on 28 July 2017, a man went to an Edeka supermarket in Fuhlsbüttler Straße in the Barmbek area of Hamburg. [2] He took a 20 cm-long kitchen knife from the supermarket shelf and used it to attack several people, killing a 50-year-old German man. [3] [4] [5] Deutsche Welle reported 6 injured in addition to the killing. [6] [4] According to eyewitnesses the man shouted " Allahu Akbar " during the attack. Prosecutors said that he had hoped to die as a martyr. [7] [8] [9]

Suspect [ edit ]

Der Spiegel reported the suspect, who was arrested at the scene, as a refugee named Ahmad A., who allegedly had contacts with the Salafist sect, as well as having psychological and drug problems. [10] He is a 26-year-old Palestinian born in the in the United Arab Emirates, [11] arrived in Germany in 2015. [12] The suspect lived in a refugee hostel where his neighbours had raised warnings "repeatedly" about him. [13] Hamburg's Interior Minister Andy Grote stated that the suspect "was known as an Islamist but not a jihadist". [14]

Citing security sources, Berlin newspaper Der Tagesspiegel reported that the perpetrator was a failed asylum seeker who was known to German police; [9] he had been added to the list of 800 suspected Islamists in Hamburg prior to the attack. [15] The news agency DPA reported that security authorities were investigating evidence the man had Salafist ties. [9] He was awaiting deportation , [2] but had not been deported because he did not have «identification and travel documents». [16] While German prosecutors claim that the attacker had a "radical Islamist" motive, investigators have not found any links to jihadist groups. [17]

Police are said to have their doubts over his confessions, and believe he may be psychologically ill. [18]
