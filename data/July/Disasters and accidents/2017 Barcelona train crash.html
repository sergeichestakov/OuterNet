On 28 July 2017, a passenger train crashed into a buffer stop and derailed at Barcelona França station in Barcelona , Spain. Fifty-six people were injured, five seriously.

Accident [ edit ]

The accident happened at about 07:15 CEST (05:15 UTC ). [1] A commuter train collided with the buffers at Barcelona França station and derailed. [2] The train was operating a Sant Vicenç de Calders – Barcelona França service on the R2 line . [3]

The train involved was an electric multiple unit of RENFE Class 465 , number 210M. [1] Witnesses stated that the train did not brake on entering the station. [4] Fifty-six people were injured, [5] five seriously. [3] Eighteen people were taken to hospital. Victims were taken to four different hospitals in Barcelona. [1]

Investigation [ edit ]

The Administrador de Infraestructuras Ferroviarias (ADIF) opened an investigation into the accident. [4] The Comisión de Investigación de Accidentes Ferroviarios (CIAF) is also responsible for investigating railway accidents in Spain. Its staff were seen on site following the accident. [6] The train driver was found not to have been under the influence of drugs or alcohol. The train event recorder was recovered from the train. [5]
