Virgen de la Asunción fire
San José Pinula
Date March 8, 2017
Location San José Pinula , Guatemala
Coordinates 14°34′35″N 90°29′52″W ﻿ / ﻿ 14.5764°N 90.4978°W ﻿ / 14.5764; -90.4978
Deaths 41 [1]
Non-fatal injuries 38 [2]

On March 8, 2017, at least 40 girls between the ages of 14 and 17 years old, were killed when a fire broke out after students captured after a mass escape staged a protest at the Virgen de la Asunción orphanage in San José Pinula , Guatemala . As they were locked down, students protested human rights conditions including alleged sexual and physical abuse by apparently setting fire to their bedding. [3]

A neighbor heard three of the girls shouting for help in committing suicide, asking for "pills, a knife or something" as they no longer wanted to live at the home". Two girls who escaped heard shouting that "they were going to sacrifice so that everyone knew what they lived in there." [4]

On March 13, Guatemala’s minister for social welfare, Carlos Rodas, offered his resignation. [5]

On March 14, the attorney general's office confirmed the arrest of Morales administration former Social Welfare Secretary Carlos Rodas, former Deputy Secretary Anahi Keller, and the former director of the Virgen de la Asuncion shelter where the deadly fire broke out, Santos Torres. [6] The suspects were accused of wrongful death, mistreatment of minors, and negligence. [7]

In June 2017, five people include two police officers and three government officials were charged of manslaughter and negligence during the incident. [8]
