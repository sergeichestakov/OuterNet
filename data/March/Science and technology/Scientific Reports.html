Scientific Reports is an online open access scientific mega journal published by the Nature Publishing Group , covering all areas of the natural sciences . In this journal a paper is not assessed based on its perceived importance, significance or impact, but rather is considered solely on its scientific validity. [1]

On 23 August 2016, a blog post on the Scholarly Kitchen mentioned that the journal is likely to become the largest one in the world, overtaking open access PLOS ONE , [2] which indeed has first occurred in September, 2016 [3] and was later confirmed in first quarter of 2017. [4]

Abstracting and indexing [ edit ]

Scientific Reports is abstracted and indexed in the Chemical Abstracts Service , [5] PubMed / MEDLINE , and the Science Citation Index . According to the Journal Citation Reports , the journal's impact factor has increased from 2.927 in 2012 to 5.228 in 2015. [6] It has since dropped down to 4.259 in 2016.

Peer review [ edit ]

Scientific Reports has been described as a mega journal , conceptually similar to PLOS ONE , based on article processing charges and a peer review model emphasizing scientific quality, rather than perceived impact. [7] Its editorial board is extremely large, with over a thousand listed members. [8] In 2015, an editor, Mark Maslin, resigned from Scientific Reports [9] when the journal introduced a trial of a fast-track peer-review service for biology manuscripts in exchange for an additional fee. [10]
