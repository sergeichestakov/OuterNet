2017 Arkema plant explosion

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation , search

The 2017 Arkema plant explosion was an industrial disaster that took place during Hurricane Harvey in Crosby, Texas . Flooding from the hurricane disabled the refrigeration system at the plant which manufactured organic peroxides . [1] [2]

According to the New York Times , this explosion raised serious issues regarding flaws in the safety standards, emergency preparedness and regulation of chemical processing plants. [3]

2017 OSHA fines [ edit ]

In February 2017, OSHA issued safety fines regarding general safety and maintenance in the sum of $91,724. [4]

Lobbying against safety rules [ edit ]

The International Business Times reported that the Trump administration's EPA administrator Scott Pruitt on March 16, 2017, blocked new chemical plant safety rules following an extensive lobbying campaign that included the American Chemistry Council . [5]

Events [ edit ]

The plant flooded up to six feet deep, refrigeration was lost on August 29 and people within a 1.5-mile radius were evacuated the following day. Ignited by spontaneous combustion, the first explosions took place around 1:00 am on August 31. [6]

As flood waters receded, officials decided to stop the waiting for the Crosby community of 2300 by igniting the remaining trailers, on Sunday, September 3, 2017. [7] [4] The evacuation zone was lifted on September 4, 2017. [8]

Investigations [ edit ]

On Thursday, August 31, 2017, the same day as the first fire, the U.S. Chemical Safety Board announced an investigation. [3] As of September 2017, this board was targeted for defunding by the Trump administration. [9]

The Environmental Protection Agency began formally asking questions of Arkema on September 7, 2017. [10]

Legal proceedings [ edit ]

In September, emergency workers who responded to the explosion filed a lawsuit against the plant for damage to their health caused by the negligence of plant management. [11] [12] [10]
