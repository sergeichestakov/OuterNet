The protests against President Rodrigo Duterte apparently started on November 18, 2016 when some militant groups targeted him due to the support of burial of late president Ferdinand Marcos . [1] Duterte had been faced various controversies received from opposition and other militant groups because of his, mainly, implementation of anti-drug campaign in the Philippines that caused thousands of suspected drug users were killed in the police operations.

Background [ edit ]

War on drugs and extrajudicial killings [ edit ]

Main articles: Philippine Drug War and Extrajudicial killings and forced disappearances in the Philippines

Since his inauguration on June 30, Duterte implemented the war against illegal drugs in the country, promising to kill thousands of people involved in the drug trade. [2] [3] This campaign was criticized by local politicians and international human rights, and was attracted by international media, due to human rights violations and high number of killings amid drug campaign.

The youngest known person killed in the drug operation is the 17-year-old student, Kian Loyd delos Santos, from Caloocan , on August 16, 2017. [4] The killing sparked the controversy among the local politicians and militant groups, and triggered massive protest in the country. [5] The family of Delos Santos, on August 25, filed murder and torture charges against police officers involved in the drug operation. [6]

Burial of Ferdinand Marcos [ edit ]

Main article: Burial of Ferdinand Marcos

On his 2016 presidential campaign , Duterte supported the burial of late President and dictator Ferdinand Marcos at the Heroes' Cemetery. [7] After Marcos eventually buried at the Heroes' Cemetery duly decided by the court, protest — organized by, mostly, youths from various universities, militant groups and local politicians — sparked in the whole country. [8] [9] [10] [11]

Marawi crisis [ edit ]

Main articles: Marawi crisis and Proclamation No. 216

The crisis in Marawi began on May 23, 2017 between Philippine government security forces and affiliated militants of the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant , including the Maute and Abu Sayyaf Salafi jihadism groups. [12] Following the clash, Duterte declared martial law in the whole Mindanao . [13]

Timeline [ edit ]

2016 [ edit ]

  • November 18 — After Marcos privately buried at the Libingan ng mga Bayani , youths gathered at various places, expressing their disappointment and rage. Not only about the Marcos family and the burial itself but Duterte is also the occasional target, mainly ranting about increasing number of killings amid war on drugs since Duterte seated as the president on June 30 . [1]
  • November 25 — Left-leaning groups called Duterte to end with Marcos family. [14]

2017 [ edit ]

  • February 18 — About thousands of Catholics people gathered in Manila , opposing the revival of death penalty and calling for an end of killings amid Duterte's war on drugs. [15] [16]
  • March 8—April 4 — Members of urban poor group, Kalipunan ng Damayang Mahihirap (Kadamay), appeal to Duterte to listen their calls for occupation of idle housing projects in Pandi, Bulacan . [17]
  • June 30 — Militant group, Bagong Alyansang Makabayan , express their disappointment for Duterte's first year in office. They call to stop airstrikes in Marawi , where the ongoing crisis took place. Instances of expressing disappointments are due to failing war on drugs, independent foreign policy and peace talks. [18]
  • July 2 — Following of the deaths of the suspected drug users in Caloocan , about 500 Catholics joined the march, calling to stop the killings. The Diocese of Caloocan said that the killings are not the solution of wiping the drugs in the country. [19]
  • July 18 — Youth groups from St. Scholastica Manila, General Santos City, and Cebu City stage a protest against Duterte for the government's ongoing war on drugs. [20]
  • July 24
    • Bagong Alyansang Makabayan led the protest on the day of his State of the Nation Address (SONA). According to Renato Reyes, secretary general of Bayan, the president’s promises of improvements during his first SONA still remained unfulfilled, including issues on contractualization , land reforms, and economic policy. [21] Also, Kilusang Mayo Uno and Alyansa ng mga Manggagawa Laban sa Kontraktwalisasyon led similar protests, calling "to resume peace talks and to end martial law in Mindanao." [22]
    • At 12 mn, around 100 anti-Duterte protesters held a candle vigil, led by Senator Risa Hontiveros , condemning the extrajudicial killings and extension of martial law in Mindanao. Hontiveros calls Duterte's second SONA a "fake". [23]
    • About 5,000 members of Bagong Alyansang Makabayan from Roxas City , 3,000 in Iloilo City , 2,500 in Kalibo , 500-800 in Cebu City, and 500 in Estancia, Iloilo joined the protest, concerning about an end to contractualization, land reform, free education and jeepney modernization. [24]
  • August 18 — Kabataan Partylist protests a ‘Black Friday,’ as youth and student groups condemn the continuing state of killings under Duterte administration. [25]
  • August 21 — A protests were held at People Power Monument and other parts of Metro Manila, in condemnation of the killing of the 17-year-old student named Kian Loyd delos Santos during the anti-drug operation in Caloocan on August 16. [26] [27]
  • August 25 — OFW groups from Saudi Arabia , London , Hong Kong , Canada , Italy , Australia , South Korea , New Zealand , Thailand and Japan protest the killing of 17-year-old Kian delos Santos and other deaths linked to the war on drugs. [28]
  • September 8 — Following the series of deaths among teens, including Carl Angelo Arnaiz and Reynaldo de Guzman (despite not related to the drug war), [29] [30] amid war on drugs, protests were held at Katipunan, Quezon City. [ citation needed ]
  • September 21 — Also called as "National Day of Protest", coinciding the 45th anniversary of declaration of Martial Law by late president and dictator Ferdinand E. Marcos, a massive protest, led by the group called "Movement Against Tyranny" — led by Bayan Muna representative Neri Colmenares , will be held at Luneta. [31] [32] Not only about the killings, but also calling out about Duterte attacks towards the institution such as the Ombudsman , and his intolerance from the critics/dissenters. [32] Malacañang Palace released Duterte’s Proclamation No. 319 and Memorandum Circular no. 26 on this day. [33] A Memorandum Circular no. 26 states that government offices and public schools at all levels are suspended. [33] Duterte said that he will not condone any means of violence committed by protesters. [34]
  • September 29 — Student activists stormed at the Polytechnic University of the Philippines president's office, occasionally target Duterte's war on drugs. [35]
