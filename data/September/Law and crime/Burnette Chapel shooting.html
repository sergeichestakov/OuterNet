On September 24, 2017, a gunman, allegedly 25-year-old Emanuel Samson, opened fire at the Burnette Chapel Church of Christ in Antioch, Nashville, Tennessee , killing one person and injuring seven others. [1]

Shooting [ edit ]

The gunman carried two guns, and after fatally shooting a woman outside, entered the church from the rear, according to police. He then walked through the church silently, shooting six more people. [2]

At this point, according to police, the gunman was confronted by an usher, Robert Engle, whom he pistol-whipped . Then Samson shot himself in the chest. Engle ran to his car to get his own gun and pointed it at Samson until police arrived to arrest him. [3] [4]

Around 50 parishioners were in the church at the time of the shooting, [5] which was reported at 11:15 a.m. local time as services were ending. [4]

Victims [ edit ]

Six people were injured by gunfire – with ages ranging from 60 to 83 – and one, Melanie Smith (aged 39) was killed. [5] An usher was also pistol-whipped during a struggle with the gunman. [4]

Suspect [ edit ]

The Metropolitan Nashville Police Department identified the suspect as Emanuel Kidega Samson, a 25-year-old Sudan native residing legally in the United States. Samson was allegedly carrying two handguns and wearing a ski mask at the time of the attack. [5] He was later charged with first-degree murder. [6] He is set to appear in Davidson County General Sessions court on Wednesday September 27, 2017, and is being held at a county jail without bond. He allegedly had additional weapons in his vehicle. [2]

Reactions [ edit ]

Mayor [ edit ]

Nashville mayor Megan Barry said "This is a terrible tragedy for our city. My heart aches for the family and friends of the deceased as well as for the wounded victims and their loved ones." [7]

Governor [ edit ]

Tennessee Governor Bill Haslam said he was "praying for the victims and committed to supporting the Antioch community in the aftermath of this tragedy." [8]

FBI [ edit ]

The FBI has launched a civil rights investigation. [8]
