On 29 September 2017, a stampede broke out at the sub-urban Prabhadevi railway station (formerly known as Elphinstone station) in Mumbai , India. [4] At least 23 people were killed and 39 others were injured in the stampede. [5] The incident took place between the Parel railway station and Prabhadevi railway station.

Incident [ edit ]

On the morning of 29 September 2017, around 10:30 am local time, a stampede erupted on a footbridge at the sub-urban Prabhadevi railway station , formerly known as Elphinstone station in Mumbai , Maharashtra , India , which connects two of Mumbai's major suburban lines. [6] [7]

The incident occurred during the morning rush hour at Prabhadevi station, when four trains arrived simultaneously at the station. [8] [9] It was further raining at the time and there were already numerous people crammed into the narrow bridge. [10] [11]

According to a Mumbai Police official, the stampede was triggered by a rumour that the pedestrian bridge was collapsing which sparked chaos among passengers as they surged forwards to flee. [12] According to a witness, the situation worsened as police and emergency officials did not respond immediately or enforce crowd control measures. [12]

At least 23 people died [13] and 39 others were injured in the stampede. According a hospital's official, most of the people who died suffered from chest compression and haemorrhage due to injuries. [14]

Reactions [ edit ]

Piyush Goyal , the Minister of Railways , announced a compensation of ₨.10 lakh or about $15,200 for the victims. [5]

President of India , Ram Nath Kovind expressed grief over the "loss of lives in the stampede in Mumbai". [15] Prime Minister of India , Narendra Modi expressed his condolences to "all those who have lost their lives due to the stampede in Mumbai", in a tweet. . [16]

Venkaiah Naidu , the Vice President; Devendra Fadnavis , Chief Minister of Maharashtra; Mamata Banerjee , Chief Minister of Bengal [17] and opposition leader Rahul Gandhi also expressed condolences over the deaths caused by the stampede. [18]

Shiv Sena called the stampede “a public massacre of the people by the government.” [19]

Many Indians took to social media and called out the government for spending on projects such as a new bullet train from Mumbai to Ahmedabad in Gujarat , instead of focusing on basic infrastructure. [11] [20] Many media reports criticised the infrastructure in the station and reported the incident as a tragedy which was waiting to happen . [21] [22] [23] [24] [25] MNS chief Raj Thackeray warned PM that he would not allow a single brick to be placed for the bullet train in Mumbai , until the infrastructure of local railways was made better. [26] [27]

In the aftermath, railway minister Piyush Goyal said that the government has ordered a probe into the incident. [28] He also ordered a safety and capacity audit of all foot over-bridges at suburban stations across Mumbai. [29]
