Ban Bueng ( Thai : บ้านบึง ; IPA: [bâːn bɯ̄ŋ] ) is a district ( amphoe ) of Chonburi Province , Thailand .

Geography [ edit ]

The district is located in the center of Chonburi Province in the Khao Khiao Massif area.

Neighboring districts are (from the north clockwise) Phan Thong , Phanat Nikhom , Bo Thong , Nong Yai , Si Racha and Mueang Chonburi .

History [ edit ]

The district was created in 1921 as a minor district ( king amphoe ). [1] 1938 it was elevated to full district status.

Administration [ edit ]

The district is subdivided into 8 subdistricts ( tambon ), which are further subdivided into 52 villages ( muban ). Ban Bueng itself has town ( thesaban mueang ) status and covers parts of tambon Ban Bueng. Nong Phai Kaeo and Khlong Kio each have township ( thesaban tambon ) status, both townships covering parts of the same-named tambon .
