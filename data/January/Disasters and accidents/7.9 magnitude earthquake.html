The 2017 Papua New Guinea earthquake was an earthquake of 7.9 moment magnitude that struck at 04:30 UTC on 22 January 2017. [1] [2] At 06:39 UTC, the Pacific Tsunami Warning Center issued a final message, indicating that "there is no longer a tsunami threat from the earthquake." [3] Three people were killed during the quake and one was injured. [4] Damage occurred in Arawa and parts of central Bougainville , while a power outage occurred in Buka . [5]
